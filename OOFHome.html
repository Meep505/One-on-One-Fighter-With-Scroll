One on One Fighter

Rules:
  1. The goal of the game is to defeat the other fighter. You and your opponent each make a move each turn simultaneously.
  2. Each fighter has 4 stats: strength, speed, cunning, and fatigue. You will always start with 6 strength, 6 speed, 6 cunning, and 30 fatigue. The computer will start with two stats randomly changed.
    • Strength- Used to calculate attack
    • Speed- Used to calculate attack and defense. Will decide who attacks first if both fighters attack in one turn.
    • Cunning- Used to calculate attack and defense if defending.
    • Fatigue- Used to determine when a fighter can do a finishing move.
  3. Each turn, a fighter makes one of 3 moves: attack, defend, or finishing move.
    • Attack- Attack your opponent, reducing their fatigue.
      • How attack is calculated: (strength + speed + cunning) / a random number 1-3
      • Damage dealt: attack - defense
      • No damage is dealt if attack < defense
    • Defend- Defend against your opponent. If both players, defend, they both recover a random number of fatigue 1-6.
      • Defense when defending: speed + cunning
      • Defense when not defending: speed + a random number 1-6
    • Finishing Move- Use this move to finish off your opponent. The move is only available under one of these two circumstances:
      • Finishing move is only available under one of these two circumstances:
        • One fighter's fatigue is double the other's
        • One fighter's fatigue goes below 0
      • How finishing move is calculated: (strength + speed) / a random number 1-3
  4. The game is won when one fighter deals more than 1 damage to the other fighter using their finishing move.
